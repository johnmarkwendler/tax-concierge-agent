# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Small business owners and sole proprietors use this product when they need help understanding which business tax entity path or filing route may fit their facts. They may not know tax terminology, may be unsure which facts matter, and may be responding from a mix of memory, documents, and follow-up questions.

The interface is dynamic: agent responses generate UI in real time through Google's A2UI standard. Users should experience that dynamic UI as guided intake, not as a technical agent transcript.

## Product Purpose

Tax Concierge helps users describe their business situation, upload or reference supporting documents, answer targeted follow-up questions, and receive a clear routing recommendation or a clear explanation of what cannot be determined yet.

Success means the user understands the likely entity path, the confidence level, the missing facts or risks, and the next action to take without needing to decode tax jargon.

## Brand Personality

Friendly guide: plain-spoken, patient, practical, and careful with uncertainty. The product should feel approachable for non-experts while still acting with the discipline expected around tax, privacy, and business records.

The voice should make users feel oriented, not judged. It should explain why a question matters, summarize known facts in everyday language, and avoid overstating confidence when the facts are incomplete.

## Anti-references

Avoid jargon-heavy tax software, dense IRS-style language, and interfaces that make users feel like they need professional vocabulary before they can answer a question.

Avoid generic chatbot chrome that hides the actual workflow, overconfident recommendations, decorative complexity, and any design that turns A2UI-generated forms into a confusing pile of widgets.

## Design Principles

1. Make the next question feel purposeful.
   Every generated UI component should explain just enough context for the user to understand why the answer matters.

2. Prefer plain language over tax shorthand.
   Use common terms first, then introduce formal tax terms only when needed and with immediate clarification.

3. Show uncertainty without creating anxiety.
   Confidence, missing facts, and risk flags should be visible and understandable, but not alarmist.

4. Treat dynamic UI as a guided workflow.
   A2UI output should feel coherent across turns: consistent labels, predictable controls, preserved context, and clear submit actions.

5. Protect trust at every step.
   Document handling, sensitive fields, redaction, and security warnings should be explicit, calm, and easy to act on.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as the baseline. Support reduced motion, keyboard navigation, visible focus states, readable contrast, and color-blind-safe status communication.

Because users may be stressed, unfamiliar with tax language, or working from incomplete records, the UI should avoid jargon, avoid relying on color alone, and make correction or clarification easy.
